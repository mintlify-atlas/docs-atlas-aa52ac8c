## Metrics

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **DER** | Diarization Error Rate — measures accuracy of speaker assignment |
| **cpWER** | Concatenated minimum-Permutation Word Error Rate — WER after optimal speaker-permutation alignment |
| **tcpWER** | Time-constrained cpWER — cpWER with time boundary constraints applied |
| **WER** | Word Error Rate — standard transcription accuracy metric |

<Note>
  Lower is better for all four metrics. DER, cpWER, and tcpWER jointly evaluate both transcription accuracy and speaker assignment; WER measures transcription accuracy alone.
</Note>

## Language distribution

VibeVoice-ASR covers **50+ languages** without requiring an explicit language flag at inference time. The model auto-detects the input language and handles code-switching natively.

Represented language families in the training distribution include:

- **Germanic**: English, German, Dutch
- **Romance**: French, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese
- **Sino-Tibetan**: Mandarin Chinese (Simplified and Traditional)
- **Japonic**: Japanese
- **Koreanic**: Korean
- **Slavic**: Russian, Polish
- **Tai-Kadai**: Thai
- **Austroasiatic**: Vietnamese
- And many more across Asia, Europe, and beyond

<Note>
  Language distribution details and the full list of supported languages are available in the [VibeVoice-ASR technical report](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.18184).
</Note>

## Interpreting results

- **DER** below 5% is generally considered strong performance for conversational meeting scenarios with 2–4 speakers.
- **WER** in the 8–15% range on multi-speaker, spontaneous speech is competitive with purpose-built ASR systems that do not perform diarization.
- The **tcpWER** metric penalizes both transcription errors and timestamp inaccuracies, making it the most demanding of the three WER variants.

<Tip>
  For domain-specific use cases with technical vocabulary or uncommon proper nouns, use the [hotwords feature](/asr/usage#using-hotwords) to improve WER on out-of-vocabulary terms.
</Tip>
